I spent about three hours working with Kimi K2.6 on OpenCode Go.

I switched to Kimi K2.6 and ran through my usual coding workflow. Here's what I found.

:::conclusion
Kimi K2.6 feels as smart as GPT-5.4 class. But it's slow. In practice, Codex is still better.
:::

## The intelligence is genuinely top-tier

The most striking thing about three hours with Kimi K2.6 was how *smart* it is.

Code design decisions, refactoring suggestions, error root cause analysis — all high quality. When I threw multi-file design changes at it, it understood the context accurately and gave thoughtful answers.

When I say GPT-5.4 class intelligence, that's what I mean. It genuinely feels like it belongs in the top tier alongside the latest models.

## But it's painfully slow

Smart as it is, there's a significant problem: **it's slow**.

Kimi K2.6 is a deep-reasoning model that takes its time. A single question can take 2-3x longer to come back compared to other models. You start developing the habit of switching to other tasks while waiting.

On OpenCode Go, Kimi K2.6 gives you 1,150 requests per 5 hours (3,450 during the 3x promotion). Combined with the slow response time, I noticed a strange phenomenon: **my usage quota barely went down**.

Three hours of work and the consumption was slow. On the flip side, that means you don't really need to worry about limits — but only because responses take so long, not because it's efficient.

## Compared to Codex

In practice, I still prefer Codex (GPT-5.1 Codex).

Codex is also smart, but its strength is the *balance* between speed and intelligence. Faster responses mean faster iteration cycles. In the same time window, you simply get more done with Codex.

Kimi K2.6 gives higher-quality individual answers, but the per-request wait time slows down your entire workflow.

## Why it's still worth having

Even though I'd pick Codex for daily use, Kimi K2.6 still has a place. Three reasons:

1. **3x promotion active** — Triple usage limits, combined with the slow consumption rate, means you effectively have unlimited quota
2. **Intelligence is genuinely top-tier** — For complex design decisions, it sometimes gave better answers than Codex
3. **Strategic switching** — Use Qwen3.6 Plus or DeepSeek V4 Flash for daily work, and switch to Kimi K2.6 only when you need a critical design decision

:::note
Kimi K2.6 isn't suited for daily driving, but it's worth switching to for those "I need to get this design decision right" moments. The slowness is acceptable, and the intelligence matches or exceeds Codex. With the 3x promotion running, there's no reason not to give it a try.
:::
