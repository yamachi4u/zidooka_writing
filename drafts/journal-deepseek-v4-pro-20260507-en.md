I've started using DeepSeek V4 Pro in OpenCode Go.

Honestly, it doesn't feel all that different from OpenAI's GPT models. The code suggestions are solid, the conversation flows naturally — I could easily use this as my daily driver without a second thought.

:::conclusion
DeepSeek V4 Pro matches GPT-class performance. The only catch: its reasoning output comes through in English.
:::

There's just one thing I haven't gotten used to yet. The **reasoning/thinking output appears in English**, even when I'm giving instructions in Japanese.

It doesn't break anything functionally, but it creates a tiny mental context switch every time it happens. Your brain is in Japanese mode, and suddenly there's English flowing through the terminal.

Other than that, it's genuinely good:

- **Natural responses** — indistinguishable from GPT in daily use
- **Code accuracy** — practical, production-ready quality
- **Cost performance** — feels excellent for what you get

The English reasoning quirk will probably get ironed out through settings or model updates eventually. For now, it's a minor annoyance at worst.

Overall, DeepSeek V4 Pro is absolutely main-tier. I'm sticking with it.
