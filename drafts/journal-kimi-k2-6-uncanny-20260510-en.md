Using Kimi K2.6, a feeling kept lingering beyond just "it's smart."

**Something about it is creepy.**

:::conclusion
Kimi K2.6's chain of self-reflection feels like watching someone mutter endlessly to themselves. There's an uncanny quality to it.
:::

## The person who won't stop talking to themselves

When you read Kimi K2.6's reasoning (thinking) output, you start feeling like you're watching someone think out loud — endlessly.

Other models like Qwen3.6 Plus or DeepSeek V4 also show their thought process, but the volume is different with Kimi. It doesn't just think one step and conclude. It goes: "Wait, but I should also consider this," "Oh, but there's another possibility," "Actually, maybe my first judgment was right after all..." — looping back through its own reasoning again and again.

In human terms, it's like sitting next to someone who mutters to themselves non-stop.

They're clearly smart. But being next to someone who mumbles continuously starts to feel unsettling after a while. That's the feeling.

## Self-reflection that chains into itself

What's most unsettling is how introspection calls for more introspection.

It reaches a conclusion: "Therefore A leads to B." But then it questions itself: "But is A really true? What evidence supports A?" And then it reasons about that question, and eventually circles back to the original conclusion — but the path back is way too long.

One round would be fine. But Kimi K2.6 does this over and over.

:::warning
An AI repeatedly validating its own thinking is technically a precision-enhancing behavior. But watching it happen continuously raises an eerie question: "Can this AI actually trust itself?"
:::

## It's creepy *because* it's smart

If Kimi K2.6 were dumb, the self-talk would be harmless. "1+1 is... 3? No wait, 2? Actually 3?" — you'd just laugh.

But Kimi is smart. The monologue itself is coherent. "There are three approaches to this design. The first... the second... the third... I'll go with the first. But considering the risk of the second..."

It's saying correct things. The logic holds. And that's exactly what makes it unsettling.

**A person who keeps saying correct things to themselves, endlessly.** That's the core of the uncanny feeling I got from using Kimi K2.6.

## Still worth using

I called it creepy, but I'm not saying don't use it.

For complex design decisions, that excessive introspection sometimes leads to the right answer. In high-stakes situations, that tangled monologue can be trusted.

It's just not suited for everyday use. Seeing that stream of self-talk every time is mentally heavy.

## Summary

- Kimi K2.6 produces long reasoning chains with recursive self-reflection
- Because the content is correct and coherent, watching it feels uncanny rather than comical
- Too heavy for daily driving, but reliable for critical decisions
- It's like "the smart person you don't want sitting next to you"

When an AI feels "human-like," it isn't always a good thing. Kimi K2.6 has the kind of human-ness of **the person who can't stop overthinking**. That was the scariest part.
